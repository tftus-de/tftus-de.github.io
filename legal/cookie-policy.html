Cookie Policy

What cookies we use and how to manage them · Think Future Technologies GmbH
On this page

    This Cookie Policy explains how Think Future Technologies GmbH (“TFT”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website https://www.tftus.de. It sets out what cookies are, which ones we use, why we use them, and how you can manage your preferences. This Policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions.

    We comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG), and the Telekommunikation-Digitale-Dienste-Datenschutz-Gesetz (TDDDG). Non-essential cookies are placed on your device only after you have given your free, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent through our Cookie Consent Banner.

    1. What Are Cookies?

    Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to recognise your device on return visits and remember your preferences or actions. Cookies cannot execute programmes or transmit viruses. We may also use similar technologies such as web beacons, local storage, session storage, and fingerprinting — all referred to collectively as “cookies” in this Policy.

    2. Legal Basis for Cookie Use

    • Technically Necessary Cookies — No Consent Required: Cookies that are strictly essential for the technical delivery of the service you have requested may be placed without prior consent under § 25(2) TDDDG.
    • Non-Essential Cookies — Consent Required: All analytics, marketing, and functional cookies require your prior, freely given consent before activation. You may withdraw consent at any time (see Section 5).
    • Legitimate Interests: Security and fraud-prevention processing may rely on legitimate interests where such interests are not overridden by your fundamental rights.

    Our Cookie Consent Banner is implemented in line with guidance from the German supervisory authorities and the European Data Protection Board. Pre-ticked boxes or inaction are not treated as consent.

    3. Categories of Cookies We Use

    3.1 Technically Necessary Cookies — These cookies are required for the Website to function and cannot be switched off. They handle session management, security, and accessibility. No consent is required.

    3.2 Analytics and Performance Cookies — These cookies collect anonymised data on how visitors use the Website (pages visited, time on page, errors). Used solely to improve Website performance. Consent required.

    3.3 Marketing and Targeting Cookies — Used to deliver relevant advertising and measure campaign effectiveness. May track activity across websites. Consent required.

    3.4 Functional and Preference Cookies — Enable enhanced features such as remembering language, region, or display settings. Set by us or third-party providers. Consent required.

    4. Managing and Withdrawing Your Cookie Preferences

    You may accept, reject, or customise cookie categories at any time. Refusing non-essential cookies does not restrict access to our Website.

    4.1 Cookie Consent Banner — On first visit you will be presented with our Cookie Consent Banner to accept all, reject all, or configure preferences by category.

    4.2 Cookie Settings Panel — Update or withdraw preferences at any time via the “Cookie Settings” link in the Website footer. Changes take effect immediately.

    4.3 Browser Settings — Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies via settings. Disabling essential cookies may affect Website functionality.

    5. Consent Records and Retention

    We record all cookie consents (timestamp, choices made, Policy version) for accountability purposes. Records are retained for three (3) years and available to regulators on request. Where consent expires or processing changes materially, we will seek fresh consent.

    6. Third-Party Cookies and International Data Transfers

    Some cookies are set by third-party providers who may process data outside the EEA, including in the United States. All such transfers are made under Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission. Data Processing Agreements are in place with all third-party providers. We encourage you to review each provider’s privacy policy for further detail.

    7. Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

    Our Website respects DNT browser signals where technically feasible. A valid DNT signal will prevent non-essential cookies from activating unless you have separately provided consent. Not all third-party tools respond to DNT signals independently.

    8. Updates to This Cookie Policy

    This Policy is reviewed quarterly and whenever there is a material change in our practices or applicable law. The current version and date appear at the top of this document. For material changes, we will notify you via a Website notice or email at least fourteen (14) days before changes take effect.
