Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal data · Think Future Technologies GmbH
On this page

    Think Future Technologies GmbH (“TFT”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy and personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and safeguard your personal data when you visit our website https://www.tftus.de, use our client portals, engage our IT services, or otherwise interact with us. We are a technology solutions provider incorporated under German law and registered with the Amtsgericht Charlottenburg under HRB 253195 B, with our registered office at Christburger Str. 23, 10405 Berlin, Germany.

    We process personal data in full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”), the Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG – Federal Data Protection Act), the Telekommunikation-Digitale-Dienste-Datenschutz-Gesetz (TDDDG), and all other applicable German and European data protection laws. This Policy applies to all individuals whose data we process, including clients, business partners, job applicants, and website visitors.

    1. Scope and Applicability

    This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data processed by Think Future Technologies GmbH through its website, client portals, mobile applications, communication channels, and in connection with the delivery of IT consulting, software development, and related services. It applies to clients, job applicants, vendors, website visitors, and any individual whose data we process in connection with our business activities in Germany and across the European Union. This Policy does not apply to third-party websites or services linked from our platform, which are governed by their own privacy policies.

    2. Personal Data We Collect

    We collect personal data directly from you when you fill out contact forms, subscribe to updates, submit job applications, or engage our services. This may include your full name, job title, company name, email address, phone number, professional background, curriculum vitae, and communication history. We also receive data automatically through your use of our website, such as your IP address, browser type, device information, operating system, and browsing behaviour (e.g., pages visited, time spent, referral source). These technical details are captured via cookies, server logs, and analytics tools to improve user experience and site performance.

    In the course of delivering services such as software development, QA testing, AI solutions, or cloud consulting, we may process project-related data provided by clients, including business contact details or system logs. We do not collect special categories of personal data (e.g., racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, health information, or biometric data) unless strictly necessary for legal compliance, security verification, or with your explicit consent pursuant to Article 9 GDPR.

    3. How We Use Your Personal Data

    We collect and process your personal data only for lawful, specific, and clearly defined purposes. Your information is used to respond to your enquiries, provide and manage contracted services, maintain client relationships, send marketing communications (only with your prior consent), conduct recruitment activities, improve our website and service offerings, enhance security, perform analytics, and meet legal or regulatory requirements.

    We process personal data only where a valid legal basis under the GDPR applies:

    • Article 6(1)(b) — Contractual Necessity: processing necessary to provide services, perform contracts, or take pre-contractual steps.
    • Article 6(1)(c) — Legal Obligation: processing necessary to comply with applicable legal, tax, accounting, employment, and regulatory obligations.
    • Article 6(1)(f) — Legitimate Interests: processing necessary for business operations, security, fraud prevention, analytics, and protection of legal rights, balanced against your rights and freedoms.
    • Article 6(1)(a) — Consent: processing based on your consent, which may be withdrawn at any time.
    • Article 6(1)(d) — Vital Interests: processing necessary to protect the vital interests of an individual in emergency situations.

    You may withdraw your consent for marketing or other optional processing at any time by contacting us at info@tftus.com, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to such withdrawal. A detailed record of all processing activities is maintained in our Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) pursuant to Article 30 GDPR, available upon supervisory authority request.

    4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

    Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to provide essential site functions, enhance user experience, analyse traffic, and personalise content. In accordance with § 25 TDDDG (formerly TTDSG) and the EU ePrivacy Directive, we only set non-essential cookies with your prior, informed, and freely given consent, obtained through our Cookie Consent Banner.

    You may accept, reject, or customize your cookie preferences at any time via the Cookie Settings panel accessible in the footer of our website. Refusing non-essential cookies does not restrict your access to the core features of our website. You may also manage cookies through your browser settings. We respect “Do Not Track” (DNT) browser signals where technically feasible. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data for cross-context behavioural advertising or any form of third-party data monetisation.

    For detailed information about the cookies we use, see our Cookie Policy.

    5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

    We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share it only with trusted third parties who support our operations, and only to the extent strictly necessary, including: cloud infrastructure and hosting providers (e.g., AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud) operating within the EU/EEA or under appropriate transfer safeguards; analytics and performance tools (e.g., Google Analytics, configured with IP anonymisation and data minimisation); payment processors (e.g., Stripe, operating under their own GDPR-compliant frameworks and bound by an Article 28 DPA); professional advisors including legal counsel, auditors, tax consultants, and Wirtschaftsprüfer, bound by professional confidentiality obligations under German law; human resources and payroll service providers operating within the EEA under binding data processing agreements; and affiliated group entities under Standard Contractual Clauses and supplementary organisational measures (see Section 6 below).

    Everyone we work with is bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) pursuant to Article 28 GDPR, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or equivalent safeguards to ensure a level of protection equivalent to this Policy. A list of our current sub-processors is maintained, updated upon any changes, and is available upon request. We may disclose data when required by law, court order, or supervisory authority, or to protect our legal rights, users, or property. In the event of a merger, acquisition, or business transfer, your data may be transferred to the successor entity under the same privacy standards, with advance notice provided to you where legally permissible.

    6. International Data Transfers

    We may transfer and process personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA). Accordingly, all transfers of personal data are carried out exclusively on the basis of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted by the European Commission pursuant to Article 46(2)(c) GDPR, in their most current form (EU Commission Implementing Decision of 4 June 2021).

    Prior to any transfer, we conduct a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) in accordance with EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 to evaluate: (i) whether the legal framework in India (including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023) provides an essentially equivalent level of protection to that guaranteed within the EU; (ii) whether supplementary technical and organisational measures, such as encryption in transit via TLS 1.2+ and at rest via AES-256, pseudonymisation, role-based access controls, and contractual audit rights, are in place to address any identified protection gaps; (iii) whether the specific categories of data transferred are proportionate to the operational purpose and do not include special categories of personal data without additional safeguards under Article 9 GDPR; and (iv) whether data transferred to India is subject to retention schedules consistent with both GDPR and DPDP Act requirements, as set out in Section 7 of this Policy.

    Our data flows with Think Future Technologies Pvt. Ltd. are governed by an intra-group data transfer and processing agreement incorporating the EU SCCs (Module 1: Controller to Controller, and Module 2: Controller to Processor, as applicable), supplemented by a binding and enforceable data protection addendum. This agreement grants TFT GmbH audit rights over the Indian entity’s data processing practices and requires mandatory incident notification within 24 hours of any suspected breach.

    Where we use third-party service providers located in countries outside the EEA, including the United States, we ensure that all such transfers are governed by appropriate safeguards including SCCs, adequacy decisions where available, or Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs). We do not transfer personal data to recipients in countries identified as high-risk by the EDPB without implementing all required supplementary measures. You have the right to obtain a copy of the applicable transfer mechanism by contacting us at the details set out in Section 16 of this Policy.

    7. Data Retention

    We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected or as required under applicable German, EU, and international law, in accordance with the principle of storage limitation under Article 5(1)(e) GDPR. Retention periods are determined based on the nature of the data, the legal basis for processing, and applicable statutory obligations.

    Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted using industry-standard deletion methods, irreversibly anonymised, or archived in a restricted-access environment where further processing is not permitted. Retention schedules are reviewed annually by the Data Protection Officer and updated as necessary.

    8. Your Data Protection Rights

    Under the GDPR and applicable German data protection law (BDSG), you have comprehensive rights over your personal data. We are committed to honouring these rights promptly, transparently, and free of charge, unless a request is manifestly unfounded, excessive, or repetitive. These rights include:

    • Right to be Informed — clear, concise, and transparent information about how your personal data is processed.
    • Right of Access — confirmation of whether we process your personal data and a copy of that data with supplementary information.
    • Right to Rectification — correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data without undue delay.
    • Right to Erasure (“Right to be Forgotten”) — deletion of your personal data where no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful.
    • Right to Restriction of Processing — temporary suspension or limitation of processing in specific circumstances.
    • Right to Data Portability — receiving your data in a structured, machine-readable format, where processing is based on consent or contract.
    • Right to Object — objecting at any time to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
    • Rights in Relation to Automated Decision-Making and Profiling — not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects.
    • Right to Lodge a Complaint — with a supervisory authority, in particular the Berliner Beauftragte für Datenschutz und Informationsfreiheit (BlnBDI), Friedrichstraße 219, 10969 Berlin — www.datenschutz-berlin.de.

    To exercise any of the above rights, please submit a verifiable written request to info@tftus.com, including your full name and sufficient information to identify you. We will verify your identity through reasonable and proportionate means and respond within thirty (30) calendar days, extendable by a further two (2) months for complex or numerous requests.

    9. Right to Withdraw Consent

    Where our processing is based on your consent pursuant to Article 6(1)(a) or Article 9(2)(a) GDPR, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time without detriment, penalty, or adverse consequence. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to the withdrawal. You may withdraw consent via email to info@tftus.com, the Cookie Settings panel in our website footer, the “Unsubscribe” link in marketing emails, or by postal request to our registered office.

    10. Children’s Privacy

    We do not target or knowingly collect personal data from children. Our website, applications, and services are intended solely for professional and business use by individuals aged 18 and above. If you are a parent or legal guardian and believe that your child under 18 has provided us with personal data without your verifiable consent, please contact us immediately at info@tftus.com.

    11. Data Security

    In accordance with Article 32 GDPR, we implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (TOMs) proportionate to risk, including: encryption of personal data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256); pseudonymisation and anonymisation where appropriate; role-based access controls and least-privilege access; multi-factor authentication for systems holding personal data; regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments; a dedicated Security Operations Centre monitoring systems 24/7; mandatory annual data protection training; and a tested Incident Response Plan.

    Should a personal data breach occur, we will move quickly to contain it, investigate the root cause, and report to the Berliner Beauftragte für Datenschutz und Informationsfreiheit (BlnBDI) without undue delay and, where feasible, within seventy-two (72) hours, as required under Article 33 GDPR. Affected individuals will be notified directly where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms, in accordance with Article 34 GDPR.
